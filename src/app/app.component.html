What is EPS(Earning Per Share) ?

Earnings per share (EPS) is the monetary value of earnings per outstanding share of common stock for a company. It is a key measure of corporate profitability and is commonly used to price stocks.[1] In the United States, the Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB) requires EPS information for the four major categories of the income statement: continuing operations, discontinued operations, extraordinary items, and net income.

PE (Price To Earning) Ratio ?
  • The ratio for valuing a company that measures its current share price relative to its per-share earnings.
  • PE is basically how much an investor pays for each rupee of profit earned
  • It is used for valuing companies and to find out whether they are overvalued or undervalued
  • According to many investors, Average PE Ratio Range: 20-25%.
  • But Please keep in mind, for different industries the fair PE will be different, i.e. for one industry 10 PE ratio will be average where for another industry 80PE will be average.
  • So we can’t say directly what is goo or Bad PE. It depends on Industry.

WHat is Intrinsic Value ?
  • Intrinsic Value is the anticipated or calculated value of a company, stock, currency or product determined through fundamental analysis.
  • It includes tangible and intangible factors.
  • It is used for valuing companies and to find out whether they are overvalued or undervalued
  • Intrinsic value is also called the real value (fair Value) and may or may not be the same as the current market value.
  • Intrinsic Value can not be same for all. It depends on person what is the fair value of share for them to buy it. Because the real market value of company can be different for different investors.
